EPIC practices

Meet Layla

My study aims to describe and document how early communication and multilingual learning occur in the early childhood programme at League of Friends of the Blind (LOFOB). Research regarding communication development for children with visual impairments (VI) or blindness is limited in general and there is a need for a greater understanding of this population’s communication needs within the South African context. Being a community based non-profit organisation, LOFOB’s practices will help Speech-language Therapists (SLTs) in South Africa better understand the communication development of children with VI/blindness. Practices of SLTs within the South African context have shifted as seen in the EPIC framework and display a greater need for intervention to be community focussed to address the needs of marginalized populations. By documenting the practices at LOFOB, SLTs will be able to work towards not only developing population-based communication practices but also innovative practices inspired by children learning with VI/blindness.
